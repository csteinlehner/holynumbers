It never rains in Jerusalem

If you spend time in Jerusalem you usually don't see any rain for month. So sometimes it feels like it never rains here. But in winter time of course there is rain and sometimes massive amounts. But especially in comparison to Berlin you have the feeling that there is not a lot precipitation in Jerusalem.

Weekly rainfall in Jerusalem and Berlin

After comparing the temperature and haven't been surprised there I was amazed how much it really rains in Jerusalem. The weekly average is roughly the same but the distribution is totally different. In Berlin it rains around the year, with small spikes in the summer. In Jerusalem you usually won't see rain from April to September, but the amount of rain during some weeks of winter are multiples of what you usually see in Berlin.

Data

The datasource for this visualization is the historical weather data from NOAA National Climatic Data Center, the same as for the temperature comparison.

For this visualization I added up weekly values to smoothen out daily variations. The data is brought to JSON format via small scripts you can download at github. The data is available for Jerusalem(json) and Berlin(json).
