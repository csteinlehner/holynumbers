The weather is nice, right?

One ongoing topic when people hear I'm in Jerusalem is the weather. Everybody in Germany is asking me how warm it is and at the same time is complaining about how bad the weather is in Germany. Of course it is the common small talk topic and for me it is just expected, that it is warm in Jerusalem. Unless it is winter and 10 degrees outside means 15 degrees in the flat and I'm freezing in front of the computer.

Temperature comparison between Jerusalem and Berlin

Looking at the data it comes at no surprise that the average temperature in Jerusalem is indeed nearly 10 degrees above Berlins temperature. It's also really uncommon to have subzero temperature in Jerusalem. But proper heating and isolation in Jerusalem houses is also not common. So in winter it often feels colder than Germany.

So, ok the data is a little bit stating the obvious, but looking at weather data like rain has some surprises.

Data

Beginning this project I thought getting historical weather data should be easy. But searching for historical data I ran into a lot of pay walls with sometimes quite expensive rates or total unclear costs. The NOAA National Climatic Data Center provides some free global historical weather data. To use this data in my visualizations I wrote small python scripts. The used data is accessible for Jerusalem (json) and Berlin(json).
